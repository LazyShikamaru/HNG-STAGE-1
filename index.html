About Me

A deeper look into who I am, where I'm going, and what I'm learning along the way.

My Story

I've always been the "tech guy" for my friends and family. I remember when the internet was still new and mysterious to many; I was the one who found it cool and dove in, learning how to find information and make things work. I enjoyed being the person who could navigate the complexity.

I bring that same spirit to my work as a developer. I know that technology can sometimes feel frustrating or confusing. That's why my mission is simple: to build things that lift a burden from your shoulders. When you interact with my work, I want you to feel taken care of, confident that it will just work, simply and reliably.

My Goals for HNG

My primary goal in this program is clear: to truly master JavaScript. For a long time, I've been caught in "tutorial hell," understanding concepts in theory but struggling to apply them. My aim is to break free from that cycle and stop my over-reliance on AI by learning to solve complex problems with my own logic.

More than just building better projects, this is about proving to myself that I can conquer difficult challenges. Successfully completing this internship will give me the confidence and the practical skills I need to pursue my ultimate ambition: securing a high-paying remote job or building a successful career as a freelance developer.

My Honest Challenges

I'll be blunt: my two biggest challenges are visual design and writing clean, effective JavaScript from scratch. I often find the design process frustrating, which is why I lean on AI to help generate the initial design system. It allows me to focus my energy on the part I enjoy more: making things work.

My main focus for improvement is on problem-solving with code. My goal is not just to write JavaScript that functions, but to write code that is so clear and logical that my future self and any other developer can understand it without a headache. That, to me, is the mark of a true professional.

A Note to My Future Self

Hey, me. I hope you're doing well. I want you to remember this moment clearly: the struggle of teaching yourself JavaScript, piece by piece, without a mentor to guide you. Remember how hard it was to learn by building, to push through the confusion on your own. That struggle is forging the discipline and resilience you now have.

Never lose the hunger to learn that's driving you right now. No matter how successful you've become, remember that consistency is what got you here. And most importantly, I hope you've continued to make friends and build connections. The code is important, but the people are everything. Don't ever forget that.

My Foundation

My foundation is my faith. It teaches me to work with diligence in all things, confident that I can overcome any challenge through Christ who gives me strength.
